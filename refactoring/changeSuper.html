Change Superclass

Forces of Conflict

Consider the situation where you have a class you've written, and would like that class to behave nearly-identically to a class in another library. However, you can't simply use an instance of that library's class due to small differences in what it can do and the contexts in which you need it to operate.

Or, maybe, there are two classes that exhibit similar behavior and have a significant amount of duplicate code.

Resolution

In the first example above, changing the class's superclass to the desired external library's class would allow you to inherit everything you'd normally need from it while making the modifications that keep it functioning in the context of your program.

In the second example, you can consolidate their duplicated methods and instance variables into a singular superclass, changing superclass to it.
